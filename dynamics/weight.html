Problem 3

(LSM P5.76) A swimmer has a mass of 80.0 kg and jumps off a board 10.0 m above the water. Three seconds after entering the water, her downward motion is stopped. What average upward force did the water exert on her?

Solution

Ultimately, we need the acceleration. To get that, we need the initial and final velocity of the swimmer in water. We know the final velocity (rest at 0m/s), but we don't know the initial velocity. To get that, we note that she fell from 10.0m; free fall.

Starting from rest, we first get the time she took to hit the water.

y=y0+v0t+12at2
(0m)=(10.0m)+12(−9.8m/s²)t2
0=−4.9t2+10

The swimmer then took 1.43s to hit the ground. Thus, we get the velocity now.

v=v0+at
v=(0m/s)+(−9.8m/s²)(1.43s)

Thus, the velocity at the water is −14.0m/s. We can now get the acceleration, given that it took three seconds to go to rest.

v=v0+at
(0m/s)=(−14.0m/s)+a(3s)

Finally, we have the acceleration at 4.67m/s². We can finally get the force.

F=(80.0kg)(4.67m/s²)

Giving us that the force of water is 373.6N.
